Why cycle count beats hour count

Two people can both sleep "8 hours" and feel completely different depending on where the alarm lands. Waking near the end of a cycle, when sleep is lightest, is what makes the difference — not the raw number of hours.

5 vs 6 cycles

5 cycles (about 7.5 hours) is often the practical minimum for feeling rested. 6 cycles (about 9 hours) gives more recovery time, especially useful after a short night or heavy training.

Falling asleep isn't instant

Most people take roughly 10–20 minutes to actually fall asleep. That gap matters — it's why bedtime and "lights out" aren't quite the same thing.
